

<h1>SPEC 02: Chromatic Load-Out Protocols</h1>

<div class="meta">
<div><strong>Standard Code:</strong> LITH-UCE-PLO-01</div>
<div><strong>Classification:</strong> Biological Chromatic Data</div>
<div><strong>System Logic:</strong> CMYK-B (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key-Black)</div>
</div>

<p>
This protocol defines the biological pigment requirements and sequestration logic for the UCE™ system.
A <strong>Load-Out</strong> consists of concentrated chromatic data adhered to the
<strong>Neutral Chromatic Base (NCB)</strong>, remaining chemically inert until the
<strong>Thermal Phase-Change Trigger</strong> is reached.
</p>

<h2>1. Primary Chromatic Engines</h2>

<p>
To achieve standardized industrial color reproduction, the system utilizes four primary biological profiles.
</p>

<h3>1.1 Cyan Protocol (Azure)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Constituent:</strong> Ultra-Refined Phycocyanin.</li>
<li><strong>Technical Requirement:</strong> Pigment must be sequestered within the Porous Polysaccharide Matrix to reduce protein denaturation above 60°C.</li>
<li><strong>Performance:</strong> High-heat stability to 180°C for 12 minutes within the laminated scaffold.</li>
</ul>

<h3>1.2 Magenta Protocol (Crimson)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Constituents:</strong> Lycopene / Betalain Blend.</li>
<li><strong>Technical Requirement:</strong> Requires Hydrophobic Lamination to reduce oxidation and browning during thermal processing.</li>
<li><strong>Performance:</strong> Maintains red-to-purple hue integrity in high-moisture systems.</li>
</ul>

<h3>1.3 Yellow Protocol (Solar)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Constituent:</strong> 95% Purified Curcuminoids.</li>
<li><strong>Technical Requirement:</strong> Lipid phase must function as a dry-state dispersant to prevent pigment clumping.</li>
<li><strong>Performance:</strong> Rapid micellar dispersion upon activation.</li>
</ul>

<h3>1.4 Key-Black Protocol (Obsidion)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Constituent:</strong> High-C3G Anthocyanins.</li>
<li><strong>Technical Requirement:</strong> Requires the Inorganic pH Governor defined in SPEC_01 to maintain stable deep-purple to black coloration in alkaline environments (pH &gt; 7.0).</li>
<li><strong>Performance:</strong> Zero-shift chromatic stability in volatile substrates.</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. Implementation Standards</h2>

<h3>3.1 Uniformity Metric</h3>
<p>
To satisfy the UCE™ Standard, blended load-outs must demonstrate
<strong>Optical Homogeneity</strong>. Particle size must remain at
<strong>D90 ≤ 5.0 µm</strong>. Failure to meet this threshold may result in chromatic streaking or visible particulate spotting.
</p>

<h3>3.2 Hardware Execution</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standard Implementation:</strong> Multi-pass mechanical blending and cryogenic sifting.</li>
<li><strong>Optimized Implementation (Aer™):</strong> Reference engine utilizes Precision Kinetic Integration to homogenize disparate load-out particles into a singular dry-phase material for advanced embodiments such as clean-label foams and high-heat batters.</li>
</ul>
