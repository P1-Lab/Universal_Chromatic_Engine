  <h1> Payload Protocols: The Chromatic SKUs</h1>

  <p>
    <strong>Standard Code:</strong> LITH-UCE-PLO-01<br/>
    <strong>Classification:</strong> Biological Load-Outs (Active Layer)<br/>
    <strong>System Logic:</strong> CMYK-B + Botanical Modifiers
  </p>

  <p>
    A <strong>Load-Out</strong> is a concentrated biological pigment payload adhered to the
    <strong>Neutral Chromatic Base (NCB)</strong>. These payloads are intended to remain
    protected until activation by heat, shear, hydration, or process-specific triggers.
  </p>

  <hr/>

  <h2>1. THE PRIMARY QUARTET (CMYK ARCHITECTURE)</h2>

  <h3>1.1 UCE-AZURE (Cyan / Blue Engine)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Phycocyanin from Arthrospira platensis (spirulina)</li>
    <li><strong>Known Challenge:</strong> Heat-sensitive protein pigment with reduced stability at elevated temperatures.</li>
    <li><strong>UCE Logic:</strong> Encapsulation may improve survivability during moderate thermal processing.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>1.2 UCE-CRIMSON (Magenta / Red Engine)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Lycopene systems, beet betalains, or blended red botanical pigments</li>
    <li><strong>Known Challenge:</strong> Oxidation, browning, and fading under heat or oxygen exposure.</li>
    <li><strong>UCE Logic:</strong> Lipid barrier systems may reduce oxygen contact prior to activation.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>1.3 UCE-SOLAR (Yellow / Light Engine)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Curcuminoids (turmeric) or related yellow extracts</li>
    <li><strong>Known Challenge:</strong> Poor dispersion and localized flavor concentration if agglomerated.</li>
    <li><strong>UCE Logic:</strong> Fine particulate dispersion can improve uniformity in emulsions and batters.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>1.4 UCE-OBSIDIAN (Key-Black / Depth Engine)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Purple corn anthocyanins, dark fruit anthocyanins, or botanical black blends</li>
    <li><strong>Known Challenge:</strong> Strong pH sensitivity causing hue shifts.</li>
    <li><strong>UCE Logic:</strong> Buffered micro-environments may improve localized color retention.</li>
  </ul>

  <hr/>

  <h2>2. THE EXPANDED SPECTRUM</h2>

  <h3>2.1 UCE-VERDANT (True Green)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Chlorophyll derivatives and yellow modifiers</li>
    <li><strong>Use Case:</strong> Bright botanical greens with improved process stability.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>2.2 UCE-EMBER (Burnt Orange)</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Annatto with red modifiers</li>
    <li><strong>Use Case:</strong> Cheese systems, pastry fillings, warm-toned bakery products.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>2.3 UCE-ROSEWOOD</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Hibiscus + beet systems</li>
    <li><strong>Use Case:</strong> Premium pinks, rosé confectionery, beverages.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>2.4 UCE-GOLD</h3>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Source:</strong> Turmeric + annatto balance</li>
    <li><strong>Use Case:</strong> Butters, laminated doughs, snacks.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>4. INDUSTRIAL IMPLEMENTATION: PROCESS SYNCHRONIZATION</h2>

  <p>
    Uniform dispersion of multiple pigment systems depends on particle-size control,
    anti-agglomeration behavior, and homogeneous blending.
  </p>

  <p>
    Standard mixers may create pigment hotspots or uneven optical appearance.
    The <strong>Aer Engine</strong> is positioned as a precision platform for
    vitrification, lamination, and consistent chromatic blending.
  </p>
